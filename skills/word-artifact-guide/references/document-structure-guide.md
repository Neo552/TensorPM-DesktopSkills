# Document Structure Guide

Read this for formal, client-facing, long, or print-oriented Word documents.

## Structure

- Put the purpose, audience, generated date, and source scope near the front.
- Start with a concise executive summary for management documents.
- Separate decisions, action items, risks, and assumptions into their own
  sections or tables.
- Use appendices for dense raw listings that would interrupt the narrative.

## Typography

- Use semantic heading levels, not manually bolded paragraphs.
- Keep body paragraphs short and left-aligned.
- Use style-based spacing between paragraphs and sections; avoid blank
  paragraphs as layout.
- Tables should fit a normal page width. Split wide tables or move raw detail
  to XLSX.

## Anti-Slop Bans

- No generic title page followed by a raw project dump.
- No decorative over-styling that makes later user editing fragile.
- No screenshots of text or tables when editable text/table structure is
  possible.
- No hidden assumptions in narrative paragraphs; label them explicitly.

## Review

For client-facing documents, ask `artifact-reviewer` to check that the document
answers the original request, separates decisions/action items/background, and
marks missing inputs instead of inventing facts.
